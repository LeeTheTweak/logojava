LOGO?

By Christopher Leveston

A logo is that unique stamp for a business. Every business will need one at some point. The logos you see to the right, or top, depending on the device you are using, are just several very popular logos of well known businesses. There are also many varieties of logos. Texts, something as simple as silhouettes, logos with characters or animals on them, the list can go on.


What I strive to explain to you is that what's important is the make a logo that's memoriable, yet, not too complex. Not that there is anything wrong with complex logos, but the trend of this era is simplification. The logos above were once very complex designs, but over the years, have turned into more simplistic designs. One is the face that these companies are recognizable, but because of the nature of graphic design today, many companies are looking for designs of this nature. Simplisty has taken over the industry.



As you can see, the Microsoft Windows logo on the left is that of an older version. You can see the sudden changes in the curves and even the little details in the name, 'Microsoft', has been taken out. Flatter colors are introduced, and the color gradient and highlights on the squares are all gone.


When designing a logo, you can have the choice to either include words or not. Or, if you want, you can omit the company's name and use just the logo for certain things. You'll see this a lot if your business has an app and the words doesn't have the room necessary to even be shown on the home screen of a mobile device. Humans recognize symbols and colors more than anything else. Our brains tend to leave out complex details and retain the simple ones.
