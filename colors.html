Color

By Christopher Leveston

Color is all so very important. Color is important when driving. The certain signs and lights assists us and our judgement on the road behind the scenes. Those "red" signs to the side of the road are red for a purpose. Those traffic lights above are "red", "yellow", and "green" for a purpose. Those colors give out certain moods when we look at them. Colors have personality, moods, and meaning.

Why does "red" carry the meaning to "stop"? If you think about it, "red" has always carried some negative meaning behind something...or like, the opposite of something. This is the personality traits of colors. By just looking at a color, we can get a certain vibe from them. Take these details into consideration when designing your brand and logo.

What do you feel or think when you see the color "yellow"? You may notice yellow as the color for caution, yield, slowdown. Again, color is giving out a message with little effort. "Green" has always been used for anything meaning, "OK", or "Good to Go", which is why green is go.

Let's say that you're charging your phone from a dead state. The colors on the charging light might go from "red", to "yellow", to "green". This is that messaging system that colors will bring to your brand.

Restaurants

You see a lot of red when it comes to restaurant brands. According to a article on medium.com, there are psychological properties related to color. The psychological property related the the color red is it stimulates and excites and relates closely to passion and energy. Like I said before, color plays a very important role when designing your brand. Take a look at these luxury brand logos. If you pay attention to many luxury brands, they have a limited amount of color to them. Usually less bright colors. More black and gold colors are present. Some of these luxury brands has logos that are just black.

You can also see the difference with the fonts. A lot of sharp edges and cursive. We'll get into fonts later on in the next section, Typography.
