Typography

By Christopher Leveston

According to britannica, Typography is the design, or selection, of letter forms to be organized into words and sentences to be disposed in blocks of type as printing upon a page. Typography is also the art of strategically placing letters a certain way. The goal is to make your restaurant's menu appealing to look at and choose from. So, even though we may have a nice logo, a catchy name, and a handful of great colors, the type and font of our business also matters more than you think.

Let's take a look at the FedEx logo. This logo doesn't have a crazy font and all, but what this brand had done was something clever. If you look over to where the 'E'and 'x' is, you may find an arrow pointing to the right. That's right, there is an arrow there! This company has used the capitalization of the font they chose to their design advantage. The arrow fits and looks very natural to the rest of the logo, almost as if it was placed there, but it is just utilizing the white space of the 'E' and 'x'. Brilliant!

As you can see above, these companies all have very unique fonts. These are just some of the examples you can do to your fonts. You can manipulate the lines of the fonts, you can add arrows and what not to the fonts, you can even do cursive like, Disney. The whole trick is to be as free as you can with your fonts, but make sure they are easy to read.

Here's the Red Lobster logo. A nice, clean, cursive font. The typography isn't much, and doesn't really have to be if it is clear and easily readable. The last thing you want is a menu that can't be read. The only thing that bad, unreadable typography will get you are zero sales...and that is a big no no in business. So, have at it and try many different fonts, styles, or, create your own fonts from scratch and then rearrange them in any shape or form you want.
